1. Robot Design Strategy Overview

Our robot was designed to be fast, stable, and effective at collecting cubes without dropping them. Mechanically, large rubber-banded wheels offered high linear speed and enhanced traction, allowing the robot to push opponents and remain stable on the elevated platform.

The passive intake system used hanging electrical wires configured as a one-way gate. These flexed inward to let blocks in but prevented them from falling out, keeping the payload secure without motors or sensors. The wide back helped guide cubes inward while stabilizing the robot.

2. Design Process Reflection

We adopted a rapid prototyping and test-driven process. Early issues included cubes interfering with wheels, which we resolved by adding cardboard guards.
